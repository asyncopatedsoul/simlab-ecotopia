# Biotope — GTM MVP Plan

*Companion to `biotope-gtm.md`. Defines the minimum viable go-to-market motion for Lights Out Games' Biotope launch.*

This document is to the GTM design what `biotope-mvp-planning.md` is to the product design: the smallest version of the motion that proves the model, with explicit decision gates between phases.

---

## 1. Scope of the GTM MVP

### What it tests

The GTM MVP is built to answer four questions, in order:

1. **Does the loop produce real engagement?** — does a real (non-network) family with a 5–10yo come back for a second session within 7 days, and a third within 30?
2. **Does organic acquisition work for our positioning?** — at zero or near-zero paid spend, can the founder's content + permaculture network + family-tech press deliver a sustainable trickle of installs?
3. **Will the audience pay for digital?** — what's the actual Free→Digital conversion rate at $9.99/mo? Where does it land relative to the 3–5%-in-30-days target?
4. **Does the hybrid kit thesis hold up at small scale?** — when we manually ship a quarterly kit to 100 LA-area households, does it produce the retention lift the business model assumes?

These four questions are answered sequentially across four phases. Each phase has go/no-go criteria; the next phase doesn't start until the current one passes.

### What's deferred

- Localization beyond English.
- Paid acquisition above ~$1K/mo experimental budget.
- Influencer partnerships beyond a small earned-media handful.
- Any UGC / modding / community surface.
- Hybrid sub outside LA.
- B-1 (Steam) / B-2 (Switch) distribution work.
- The 13+ scenarios and the Junior Research Station kit.
- A second product market (educators / schools / parks-as-buyers).

### What's "MVP" about this

This isn't the cheapest possible GTM — it's the **smallest one that produces real signal**. A landing page with no playable product produces no signal; a paid-acquisition blast with no organic baseline produces noise. The cadence below is calibrated to produce meaningful evidence at each gate within a quarter.

- App Store + Play certifications **approved**.

If any gate fails: extend Phase 0 by one month rather than soft-launching weak product. The first impression is unrecoverable.

## 3. Phase 1 — Soft Launch (months +1 to +3)

**Goal:** validate organic acquisition + product retention in one geography with the free tier only.

### Geography

**Canada** as primary recommendation. English-speaking, friendly App Store editorial, smaller absolute volume so issues are recoverable, mature app market with mature user behavior, geographic similarity to US for content fit. New Zealand is the alternate.

### What ships

- Free tier only — sandbox, three free scenarios, one region pack.
- App Store + Play Store live in Canada.
- PWA at biotope.app live globally (no marketing yet outside CA).
- No subscription tiers visible.
- Full attribution wired (mParticle / AppsFlyer alumni network — Mike does this himself).

### Activities

- Press at launch: press kit out to ~30 outlets the day of launch; podcast episodes drop on schedule.
- Founder content: launch announcement essay + every-2-weeks reflection essay.
- Short-form video: 1–2 per week, founder-produced.
- Light influencer outreach: 5–10 family/outdoors creators with warm pitches; no paid placements.
- App Store optimization: weekly iteration on screenshots, copy, keyword targets.
- One direct outreach to Apple's editorial team via an existing contact for Today / Kids feature consideration.

### Instrumentation

- **Acquisition attribution** wired from day one. AppsFlyer or Adjust (Mike's choice given background); mParticle as the customer-data-platform layer.
- **Funnel events**: install → first session → first scenario complete → first field activity completed → first re-encoding → 7-day return → 30-day return.
- **Cohort retention** by acquisition channel.
- **Qualitative**: quick-poll (one question per week) in-app to a sample of users; outbound email to 20 random users per week for 5-minute interviews.

### Phase 1 success criteria

- **2,000–5,000 installs** by month +3 (organic only, in one country).
- **40%+ install→first-session completion rate**.
- **30%+ first-session→7-day-return rate**.
- **15%+ 30-day retention**.
- **At least one Apple editorial placement** (Today, Kids, or local feature).
- **At least three earned-media press hits** (game/family/ecology press).

If retention is below 15% at 30 days, the product itself isn't ready for monetization — extend Phase 1, iterate the loop, before launching the subscription. **This is the most important gate.** A bad subscription launch is permanently expensive; a delayed one is recoverable.

### Phase 1 budget envelope

| Category | Estimate |
|---|---|
| Server + bandwidth | $0.5–1K/mo |
| Continued content production (2 new scenarios) | self-funded time + $4–6K contractor |
| Press / PR retainer (optional, light) | $0–3K/mo |
| Tooling | ~$200/mo |
| **Total Phase 1 (3 months)** | **$8–15K** |

## 4. Phase 2 — Digital Subscription Launch (months +4 to +6)

**Goal:** turn on monetization, expand to US/UK/AU, validate Free→Digital conversion economics.

### What ships

- Subscription added at $9.99/mo or $79.99/yr.
- 7-day free trial on first subscribe.
- Family sharing (4 child profiles + 2 parent profiles).
- 10–15 digital scenarios live (5 free + 5–10 subscriber-only across 2 packs).
- US + UK + Australia stores live; Canada continues.
- Initial paid acquisition experiment: $500–2,000/mo on Apple Search Ads only.

### Activities

- Subscription launch announcement with personal founder narrative — "what I learned in the Canadian soft launch."
- Continued podcast circuit; second wave of 5 interviews.
- Affiliate / partnership conversations begin: 2–3 family-tech newsletters, 1–2 homeschool curriculum directories.
- Paid acquisition: Apple Search Ads only at first; explicit attribution test against organic baseline.
- New content cadence holds: 2 new scenarios per month minimum.

### Instrumentation

- **Conversion funnel**: free user → paywall view → trial start → trial-to-paid conversion → month-2 retention → month-3 retention.
- **LTV by cohort and channel**.
- **CAC by channel**, including blended organic CAC (waitlist + content marketing time-equivalent dollar value).
- **Churn analysis**: when do subscribers cancel? After which scenario? Trial-to-paid drop-offs?

### Phase 2 success criteria

- **Free→Digital conversion ≥ 3% within 30 days** (target 4–5%).
- **Trial-to-paid conversion ≥ 50%**.
- **Month-2 subscriber retention ≥ 70%**.
- **Blended LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1** (organic-heavy at this stage; paid is small).
- **Digital subscriber count: 500–1,500** by month +6.

If conversion is below 2%, the paywall, scenario set, or onboarding has a problem — pause paid spend, iterate, re-test.

### Phase 2 budget envelope

| Category | Estimate |
|---|---|
| Paid acquisition (Apple Search Ads) | $1.5–6K total over 3 months |
| Content production (4–6 new scenarios) | $10–18K (mix of self + contracted) |
| Server + bandwidth | $1–2K/mo |
| Tooling + analytics | ~$500/mo |
| **Total Phase 2 (3 months)** | **$15–30K** |

## 5. Phase 3 — Hybrid Pilot (months +7 to +12)

**Goal:** validate the hybrid subscription thesis at small scale with manual fulfillment in LA.

### What ships

- Hybrid Subscription tier added at $24.99/mo or $249/yr — **LA-area only**.
- First quarterly kit assembled and shipped manually by founder from LA.
- 3 hybrid-only scenarios (the Q1 *Magnifier Mysteries* pack).
- Geographic restriction enforced at signup (zip code check).
- Cap on subscribers: **first 100, then waitlist**. Demand validation, not scale.

### Activities

- Hybrid launch as a personal founder story: "I'm shipping kits from my apartment, here's why."
- Direct outreach to LA-area parks, museums, nature centers for cross-promotion (Theodore Payne, Kidspace, Eaton Canyon, LA Audubon, Descanso Gardens).
- LA-area press: LA Times tech, LAist, LA family-tech newsletters.
- Per-shipment documentation: photo content for social, founder-narrated unboxing reactions.

### Operations

| Task | Cadence | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Source & receive components | Once per quarter | Founder + Olaunch network |
| Quality-check, kit, pack | Once per quarter | Founder |
| Print labels, print field journal pages | Once per quarter | Founder + local print shop |
| Ship via USPS Priority | Once per quarter | Founder |
| Customer service (2–5 inquiries/week expected) | Continuous | Founder |
| Component cost tracking, refine COGS | Continuous | Founder |

Estimated founder time per shipment cycle: 30–50 hours over 2 weeks (including customer service ramp).

### Phase 3 success criteria

- **100 paying Hybrid subscribers** within 3 months of launch.
- **First quarterly retention (Q1→Q2): ≥ 80%** (hybrid retention should beat digital meaningfully).
- **NPS ≥ 50** from first-shipment subscribers.
- **Validated COGS within projection**: $6–9 landed cost per kit.
- **Customer service load < 10 hours/week** (to be tractable solo).

If retention is below 60% Q1→Q2, the kit isn't carrying its weight — investigate (is the kit too simple? not durable? not used?). If COGS is above $12, pricing or sourcing needs work.

### Phase 3 budget envelope

| Category | Estimate |
|---|---|
| Initial component sourcing (test orders) | $1–2K |
| First 250 kits at scaled cost | $2–3K |
| Packaging, shipping supplies | $1–2K |
| Branded field journal printing (250 copies) | $1.5–3K |
| LA-only paid acquisition test | $1–2K/mo × 3 = $3–6K |
| **Total Phase 3 (6 months)** | **$10–17K** |

## 6. Cumulative GTM MVP Budget

| Phase | Months | Budget range |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 0 (pre-launch) | -6 to 0 | $23–39K |
| Phase 1 (soft launch) | +1 to +3 | $8–15K |
| Phase 2 (digital sub launch) | +4 to +6 | $15–30K |
| Phase 3 (hybrid pilot) | +7 to +12 | $10–17K |
| **Cumulative through month +12** | | **$56–101K** |

This is roughly the cost of a year of one senior product hire — and it produces a functioning subscription business with both digital and hybrid tiers at small scale, ready to scale on validated unit economics.

Funding sources, in order of preference:

1. **Founder self-funded** — viable for the lower bound; preserves equity and decision-making.
2. **Friends & family / angel round** — $100–250K seed for the upper bound; aligns with the founder narrative and indie-friendly investor types.
3. **Niche-aligned grants** — solarpunk / ecology / family-tech grant pools (e.g., New York Foundation for the Arts, Mozilla Builders, Patagonia's environmental grants, family-tech accelerators like Reach Capital's seed program). Long lead time but on-brand.
4. **Pre-orders / Kickstarter for the hybrid kit** — possible Phase 0 boost; on-brand for the indie-toy lineage. Risk: shifts focus from product to fulfillment promises.

**Recommended path:** founder self-funded through Phase 1 with a small bridge from family/friends if needed; structured angel round of $250–500K opened during Phase 2 once trial-to-paid conversion data is in hand.

## 8. Decision Gates Summary

The four critical decision gates, their criteria, and what happens if they fail:

| Gate | Phase | Criterion | If passes | If fails |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **G1: Alpha retention** | End of Phase 0 | 75%+ alpha retention to 7-day repeat | Soft launch in Canada | Extend Phase 0; iterate loop; do not soft launch |
| **G2: Organic + product retention** | End of Phase 1 | 15%+ 30-day retention; 2K+ installs organically | Add subscription | Extend Phase 1; iterate; do not turn on monetization |
| **G3: Conversion economics** | End of Phase 2 | 3%+ Free→Digital conversion in 30 days | Pursue hybrid pilot | Pause monetization expansion; re-examine paywall and onboarding |
| **G4: Hybrid retention** | End of Phase 3 | 80%+ Q1→Q2 hybrid retention | Begin hybrid scale (Phase 4+) | Pause hybrid; iterate kit composition; consider digital-only |

Each gate has a quantitative threshold and a qualitative honest-look. Pass both or extend the phase.

## 9. Risks & Specific Watch-Outs for the GTM MVP

- **Founder energy.** 18 months is a long time. Plan the burst weeks and the rest weeks honestly. Quarterly week-off is non-negotiable.
- **App Store editorial luck.** The single biggest organic acquisition lever is Apple's Today / Kids feature placement. It's partly meritocratic and partly relationship-driven. Mike's network helps; the asset bar must be high.
- **Permaculture / homeschool community misfit.** These are tight communities — get one early evangelist wrong and the well's poisoned. Approach via warm intros only; let the credentials speak.
- **The hybrid sub legal/operational question.** Apple's stance on physical-goods subscription routing is the single largest unit-economics uncertainty. Resolve before Phase 3 begins.
- **Audience misread.** The 5–8 vs 9–12 sub-segments may convert at very different rates. Phase 1 instrumentation must split by child age cohort to avoid averaging away signal.
- **Voice acting authenticity.** A wrong-feeling parent-narrator voice can sour the whole product. Audition more than feels necessary; commit only when there's a clear winner.
- **Founder narrative dependency.** The marketing leans heavily on Mike's personal story. If Mike steps back from press for any reason (day-job conflict, personal), the marketing has no backup voice. Plan a co-founder / spokesperson identification by Phase 3.

## 10. Open Questions / Pre-Launch Decisions

1. **Lights Out Games brand consolidation.** Domain, social handles, blog continuity — what's the brand stack for Biotope-the-product within Lights Out Games-the-publisher?
2. **App Store hybrid-sub routing legal.** External billing for the physical portion vs all-Apple. *This is the critical unblocker for Phase 3.*
3. **Soft-launch geography final pick.** Canada vs NZ vs Australia. Argument for Canada has been laid out above; finalize.
4. **Apple editorial relationship engagement.** Identify the warmest existing intro to Apple's Kids editorial team; engage 4–6 weeks before Phase 1.
5. **Hero-animation contractor short-list.** Three names from the Super Jump Games / ex-EA-THQ-Kabam network. Engage one by month -4.
6. **Voice-over short-list.** 5 candidates auditioned by month -3.
7. **Alpha households recruitment.** Identify 10 LA-area families from personal network for the closed alpha; identify 30 national for the extended alpha (permaculture / homeschool / family-tech networks).
8. **First press relationships.** Build a 30-outlet press list with one named reporter per outlet by month -2.
9. **Funding decision.** Founder-funded vs angel round vs grant pursuit. Decide before Phase 0 ends; the bridge might be needed for Phase 2.
10. **Founder hours-per-week commitment.** Honest answer drives every timeline. The current plan assumes ~25 hrs/week through Phase 0 and ~30 hrs/week through Phase 3 (with day-job continuing). Slip this and slip everything.
